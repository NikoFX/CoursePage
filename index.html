About the course

Sometimes movies are more complex than four-volume novels. But we learn to read books from school, while we don't learn to watch movies. At the same time, it can be difficult to enjoy a movie without preparation.

In this 8-session course, we will learn to be mindful viewers. We will become familiar with the stages of film production and learn how to watch movies through the eyes of a screenwriter, director, and editor. We'll explore the peculiarities of genres and examine important films, from Citizen Kane to The Matrix.

What will we learn

  • Who does what in the filming crew;
  • The stages of film production;
  • What a movie script looks like and how it changes during filming;
  • How directors use different techniques to make us laugh, cry, and feel emotions;
  • How framing, music, and lighting help convey meanings in films;
  • The different genres in cinema and how they impact the work of cinematographers, lighting technicians, and editors;
  • How to understand what a movie is about, armed with this knowledge.
